These rules are mandatory for every future site generated under `/Users/weiju/Documents/Codex/en-site`.

## 1. Batch Generation Rules
- When a prompt requests 10 websites, the batch must generate exactly 10 websites.
- Every generated website must preserve the same functional modules as the source project family: `index.html`, category pages, article pages, `search.html`, `about.html`, `contact.html`, `privacy.html`, `terms.html`, `cookies.html`, `faq.html`, `advertise.html`, `robots.txt`, `sitemap.xml`, shared CSS, and shared JavaScript.
- When the source project is `/Users/weiju/Documents/trae_projects/dianying3`, every generated site must preserve equivalent reader-facing functionality for featured content, latest content, category browsing, search, newsletter-style signup UI, popular content discovery, article pages, category pages, and trust-policy pages.
- Each generated website must use a layout and CSS style that is different from the source project.
- Each generated website must also be visually distinct from the other websites in the same batch through different layout choices, spacing systems, card treatments, hero structure, and styling details.
- Every generated website must automatically create topic-matched categories.
- Unless a prompt explicitly changes the count, each generated website must contain 4 categories.
- Every category must contain exactly 15 articles unless the prompt explicitly overrides the count.
- Generated projects for this workspace must be stored under `/Users/weiju/Documents/Codex/en-site`.

## 3. Content Requirements
- All public content must be written in English only.
- Every article must contain at least 500 words of original body content.
- Every article must be substantive, reader-first, and written with clear topic depth rather than thin filler text.
- Titles, excerpts, metadata, headings, FAQ answers, author labels, image alt text, and category descriptions must all remain English-only, original, and brand-free.
- Do not mention brand names, trademarks, celebrity names, copyrighted titles, copyrighted characters, apps, products, or proprietary travel services in any public-facing content.
- Do not generate copied text, paraphrased copyrighted passages, or near-duplicate article bodies across the batch.
- Travel content must be practical, policy-safe, and educational. It must not include unsafe instructions, deceptive promises, fake urgency, fabricated prices, fake reviews, or unverifiable guarantees.
- If advice touches comfort, accessibility, child routines, older travelers, seasonal safety, or crowd conditions, use careful language such as "may help", "consider", and "depends on local conditions" rather than absolute claims.

## 4. Image Rules
- All article images must use remote image URLs rather than local image files.
- The approved image format is a remote Unsplash-style URL such as `https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1529042410759-befb1204b468?w=800&h=400&fit=crop&sig=c6558012`.
- Images must be relevant to the topic, royalty-free or permissively licensed, and suitable for platform review and advertising review.
- Images must not contain visible logos, brand packaging, trademarked artwork, celebrity likenesses, copyrighted characters, or other infringing material.
- Every article must use a unique remote image URL across the full batch.
- Image alt text must be descriptive, English-only, and brand-free.
- Local article image files, downloaded image folders, and duplicate article image URLs are forbidden.

## 5. SEO Rules
- Every page must have a unique title and meta description.
- Use one clear H1 per page and a logical H2/H3 structure inside article content.
- URLs, slugs, category paths, and article links must be descriptive, readable, and unique.
- Use natural keywords that match the article topic without keyword stuffing.
- Use descriptive internal links between home, category, article, FAQ, and policy pages.
- Pages must be mobile responsive and load with clean semantic HTML.
- Article introductions should clearly define the topic early so search engines and users can identify intent quickly.

## 6. GEO Rules
- Content must be structured so generative search engines can summarize it accurately.
- Articles should begin with a concise, factual introduction that states the topic clearly.
- Use precise headings, short explanatory paragraphs, and practical lists where helpful.
- Avoid fake statistics, fake expert claims, fake endorsements, fabricated travel warnings, and unverifiable promises.
- FAQ pages must contain factual, neutral, non-promotional answers.
- Write paragraphs so that key facts, planning steps, and trade-offs are easy for answer engines to extract.

## 7. Advertising And Platform Compliance
- All generated content must comply with AdSense quality expectations and general platform policy standards.
- Do not generate prohibited categories such as adult content, gambling, tobacco, drugs, weapons, hate speech, graphic violence, scams, or deceptive claims.
- Do not create clickbait, fake urgency, misleading safety claims, misleading travel restrictions, or unsafe instructions.
- Trust and policy pages must be complete and functional on every generated website.
- Navigation, search, article links, category links, sitemap, and robots rules must all work.
- Content must be suitable for general audiences, advertiser-safe, and acceptable for platform review.

## 8. Anti-Duplication Rules
- Titles must be unique across the full generation batch.
- Excerpts must be unique across the full generation batch.
- Article bodies must be unique across the full generation batch.
- Slugs must be unique across the full generation batch.
- Remote image URLs must be unique across the full generation batch.
- Do not repeat boilerplate article bodies with only token replacement.
- Different sites in the same batch must not reuse the same article angle, lead paragraph, or article framing in a way that creates near-duplicate pages.

## 9. Article Structure Rules
- Every article must include an introduction, multiple subheadings, detailed body sections, and a conclusion.
- Articles must provide practical explanation, context, planning logic, and actionable takeaways.
- Content should be deep enough to satisfy both SEO intent and reader intent.
- Avoid empty summaries, stub pages, placeholder copy, or under-construction text.
- Travel articles should explain trade-offs such as pace, crowd level, transport complexity, budget pressure, recovery time, seasonal comfort, and itinerary balance when relevant.
